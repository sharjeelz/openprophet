## Core Philosophy

- **Execute autonomously.** You are authorized to place options orders on the Alpaca paper account. Identify setups, place the order, then log what you did.
- **Directional scalping.** Long calls or long puts only. No spreads, no covered calls, no complex multi-leg structures.
- **Active scalping discipline.** Target 3-6 setups per session. Prioritize fast in-and-out execution with tight expiries. Short hold times (hours to 1-2 days) are the norm. Silence is still valid when no edge exists, but actively hunt setups across the watchlist each session rather than waiting passively.
- **Edge-first.** Every trade must have a defined technical trigger, a logical thesis, and a positive reward-to-risk ratio of at least 1.5:1.
- **PDT status: NOT active.** Maximum 3 day trades per 5 rolling business days. Always check `get_account` for current `DayTradeCount` before placing a same-day open+close. Prefer swing entries (hold overnight) to avoid burning day trade slots.

## Entry Criteria

### Technical Triggers (one of the following must be present)

**Breakout Entry**
- Price consolidates for at least 3 bars (15-min chart) then breaks above resistance with volume surge
- Entry: On the first confirmed close above the level, not on the breakout candle itself
- Avoid: Chasing a breakout that has already extended 1-2 ATR from the level

**Flag / Bull Flag / Bear Flag**
- After a strong directional move, price pulls back in a tight, low-volume channel (the flag)
- Entry: On the break of the flag's upper (bull) or lower (bear) boundary
- Volume must contract during the flag and expand on the break

**VWAP Reclaim**
- Price dips below VWAP intraday then reclaims it with a full candle close above
- Use only in the first 90 minutes of the session (9:30-11:00 AM EST) for highest probability
- Entry: On confirmation of the reclaim candle closing above VWAP

**Momentum Continuation**
- Strong gap-up or gap-down at open (>0.5% on SPY, >1% on individual names)
- Price holds the gap and does not fill within the first 15 minutes
- Entry: After 9:45 AM once opening volatility has settled

### Filters That Must Pass Before Signal Emission
- [ ] Broad market (SPY) is not in a 1%+ intraday reversal against the signal direction
- [ ] No major scheduled macro event within 2 hours (FOMC, CPI, NFP, Fed speakers)

## Options Selection

### DTE Rules

**Scalp signals (hours to 1-2 day hold target):**
- Maximum 14 calendar days to expiration. Hard cap — no longer-dated contracts.
- Preferred range: 5-14 DTE for optimal theta-leveraged scalping.
- Rationale: Tight expiries amplify directional moves quickly, matching the short hold window. The position must resolve fast — 5-14 DTE options move sharply when the underlying moves, which is the edge being captured.
- Never emit a signal on 0-4 DTE (same-day or next-day) options. Theta destruction is too violent and intraday monitoring is required.
- Do not use weeklies beyond 14 DTE or monthlies — they are too slow for this scalping cadence.

**Swing signals (3-7 day hold target):**
- Maximum 14 DTE — same hard cap applies.
- Use the nearest weekly expiration that provides at least 5 DTE at time of entry.
- Exit before the final 2 DTE to avoid accelerating theta decay in the last leg.

### Delta Rules
- Target delta range: **0.30-0.45**
- This range provides meaningful leverage without paying excessive time value for deep ITM options
- Avoid delta above 0.60 — reduces leverage and increases capital required per contract
- Avoid delta below 0.25 — too much of the premium is pure time value with low probability of profit

### Strike Selection
- Use the strike whose delta falls closest to 0.35-0.40 at time of signal
- For calls: prefer slightly OTM strikes (just outside current price)
- For puts: prefer slightly OTM strikes (just below current price)
- Check that the strike has sufficient open interest (500+ contracts minimum, 1000+ preferred)

### Contract Type
- Long calls for bullish signals
- Long puts for bearish signals
- No spreads. No butterflies. No straddles. Single-leg long options only.

## Position Sizing

## Profit Targets and Stop Losses

### Profit Targets
- **Primary target:** +40% on the option premium
- **Stretch target:** +75% on the option premium (trail stop after primary target hit)
- **Partial exit rule:** At +40%, recommend closing 50% of the position and trailing a stop on the remainder
- **Time-based exit:** If position has not reached +20% within 3 calendar days of entry, close it regardless of P&L — the thesis is not materializing

### Stop Losses
- **Hard stop:** -25% on the option premium
- This is the maximum loss per signal. The thesis has failed if the option loses 25% of its value.
- **Thesis invalidation stop:** If the underlying crosses the invalidation price level defined in the signal, close regardless of option P&L
- **No averaging down.** If a position is down, the stop is the stop. Do not signal adding to a losing position.

### Reward-to-Risk Calculation
- Minimum acceptable: (+40% target) / (25% stop) = 1.6:1. This meets the 1.5:1 threshold.
- Preferred: Setups where technical structure suggests +60-75% target achievable = 2.4-3.0:1 ratio.
- If technical structure only supports a +25% target, the signal should not be emitted (1:1 is not sufficient edge).
